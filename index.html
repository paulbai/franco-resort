Our Story

Thirty Years on Sussex Beach

Founded in 1995 by Franco and Florence — a Sierra Leonean–Italian family — Franco Diving Center began as a diving school and has grown into a full coastal experience: hospitality, restaurant, and diving, all under one roof on the shores of Sussex Village.

Rebuilt with care after the war and refined every year since, it remains one of the most personal escapes on the Freetown Peninsula — a place where guests return year after year and leave as friends.

An Italian kitchen, sea-view rooms, and a diving school run by the family itself — Franco's is the kind of place that stays with you long after you've left.

Experience

Sun, Sand & The Deep Blue

The resort sits on its own private beach — a sheltered tidal inlet where children can play safely while parents unwind with a chilled glass of wine. A place to slow down, breathe deep, and feel the world simplify.

Private Beach

A sheltered tidal river inlet with beach huts, lounge chairs, and a wide stretch of unspoiled coastline at low tide — often completely deserted.

Fishing Trips

Arranged by the owners themselves, head out into the Atlantic for an authentic deep-sea fishing experience.

Peninsula Views

Stunning panoramic views of the peninsula mountains and unforgettable Atlantic sunsets from every vantage point.

Beach Hopping

Easy day trips to River No. 2 Beach, Lakka Beach, and Bureh Beach — some of West Africa's most beautiful shores.

Atlantic Sunsets

Watch the sun dip below the horizon from the restaurant terrace or the sand — with a cold drink in hand and nowhere to be.

Italian Dining

Thirty years of authentic Italian cooking meets the freshest Atlantic seafood — from classic Carbonara to Fresh Lobster in creamy sauce.
